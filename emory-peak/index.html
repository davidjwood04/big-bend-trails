Trail Details

About This Trail

Emory Peak is the highest point in Big Bend National Park at 7,825 feet — and reaching it is a legitimate all-day commitment. The route follows the Pinnacles Trail from the Chisos Basin Trailhead, gaining 2,470 feet over 5 miles before a final exposed class-2 scramble to the true summit.

The views from the top are unmatched anywhere in Texas. On clear days you can see 150+ miles in every direction: the Rio Grande, the Fronteriza mountains deep in Mexico, and the vast Chihuahuan Desert stretching north toward the Davis Mountains. Plan the summit for before noon — afternoon thunderstorms are a real hazard at exposed elevation even in October.

The final 50-100 feet to the true summit involves class-2 scrambling on exposed rock. This section is optional but achievable for most Scouts with good footwear and steady nerves. Those who skip it still get essentially the same 360° view from the false summit just below.

⚠ Strenuous rating is real. This is the hardest trail in the park. All participants should have prior hiking experience. Pre-trip fitness prep is recommended. No one should attempt this hike undertrained or underhydrated.
Pacing Guide
  • Mile 0 Chisos Basin Trailhead (5,399 ft) — Start at the main Basin trailhead behind the lodge. Restrooms and water available here. This is the last water on route — fill up. Plan a 6 AM departure for a summit by 10–11 AM.
  • Mile 0.6 Laguna Meadow / Pinnacles Junction — Bear right onto the Pinnacles Trail. The trail steepens noticeably from here. Good first check-in point for the group — anyone not feeling well should turn back now rather than later.
  • Mile 2.5 Pinnacles Pass (~6,900 ft) — Significant rest point with dramatic views of the Casa Grande formation. Approximately halfway. Nutrition and water break here. Trail grade eases slightly before the final push.
  • Mile 4.0 Emory Peak Junction — Leave the Pinnacles Trail and turn onto the Emory Peak spur. The junction is signed. This is where the terrain gets rockier and more exposed. Slow down and group up.
  • Mile 4.7 False Summit / Main Viewpoint (~7,750 ft) — Breathtaking 360° views. Most groups stop here. There are rock perches for the whole group to sit. 20–30 minutes for views, photos, and lunch before descent.
  • Mile 5.0 True Summit (7,825 ft) — Class-2 scramble on exposed rock, not technical but requires three points of contact at times. Optional for Scouts. Adults should spot younger Scouts if attempting. Return the same way to the Basin.

Troop Logistics

This is a 6 AM departure hike, no exceptions. The round trip takes 6–8 hours moving steadily with a group. Afternoon storms are a serious risk at summit elevation — everyone should be off the exposed peak by 1 PM at the latest. Weather can change quickly in October at 7,800 feet.

Water: minimum 3 liters per person, ideally a hydration pack rather than bottles. Bring real food — sandwiches, not just trail bars — for the summit break. Electrolytes matter on a day this long. Designate a front and rear adult leader. Any Scout who wants to turn back at any point should be accompanied back to the trailhead by an adult.

Post-hike plan: hot food and early bedtime. The basin store closes at 6 PM. The Chisos Mountains Lodge restaurant is open for dinner — consider reserving ahead for a group this size.

Scout Tips
💧 3L minimum. Hydration pack preferred. No water on trail — fill up completely at the Basin.
⏰ 6 AM start. Summit by 10–11 AM. Off exposed ridge before 1 PM. Non-negotiable.
🌩️ October storms still happen at 7,800 ft. Check the NPS forecast the night before. No metal poles at summit.
🥾 Trail runners or hiking boots with ankle support. The scramble section requires good grip soles.
🧊 Temperature at summit can be 20°F colder than the Basin. Pack a layer even if it's warm at the start.
🍱 Pack a real summit lunch, not just snacks. You'll be out 6–8 hours and need sustained energy on the descent.
