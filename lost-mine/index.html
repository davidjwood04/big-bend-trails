Trail Details

About This Trail

Lost Mine Trail is the crown jewel of the Chisos Mountains — a 4.8-mile out-and-back that rewards every step with increasingly dramatic views. Starting at Panther Pass (4,699 ft), the trail climbs steadily through pinyon-juniper woodland and open rocky ridgeline to the Lost Mine ridge at 5,795 ft.

The views from the top are staggering in every direction: the Sierra del Carmen glowing rust-orange to the east, Casa Grande peak looming to the south, and the Santa Elena Canyon cut faintly visible on clear days. Sunrise start is strongly recommended — you'll hit the eastern-facing ridge just as the morning light catches the Mexican peaks across the border.

For a Scout troop, this is the ideal introductory Big Bend hike. It's challenging without being brutal, the trail is well-defined throughout, and the payoff per mile is unmatched in the park. Figure on 2.5 hours moving with a group; 3 hours with breaks and photos.

Pacing Guide
  • Mile 0 Panther Pass Trailhead (4,699 ft) — Park at the pull-off on Basin Road (MM 5.1). Restrooms available at the Chisos Basin Visitor Center 1 mile back. Fill water here; there is no water on trail.
  • Mile 0.5 First viewpoint — The trail opens to a broad view of the Basin below. Good first water check for the group. Pinyon pines begin here — shade is your friend on the return trip.
  • Mile 1.1 Saddle junction — Flat spot before the ridge proper. This is the last comfortable rest area before the final push. Group up here and do a headcount before continuing.
  • Mile 1.8 Ridge traverse — Trail narrows along the ridge with exposure on both sides. Stay on trail. Views of the Sierra del Carmen appear dramatically to the east. Wind picks up significantly here.
  • Mile 2.4 Lost Mine Ridge Summit (5,795 ft) — The unofficial end point with 360° views. Rock outcroppings make natural seating. Allow 20–30 minutes here. Return via the same trail — the downhill is faster but requires steady footing on loose sections.

Troop Logistics

With a group, leave the Chisos Basin by 7:00 AM at the latest for a sunrise start at the ridge. The drive from the Basin to Panther Pass is about 10 minutes. Start early enough to be at the summit before 10 AM — afternoon heat and afternoon storms both become factors later in the day, even in October.

Water planning: each Scout should carry at least 2 liters for this hike. There is zero water on trail. For a group of 20+, designate two adults to sweep from the back and two to hold pace at the front. The trail does not have technical scrambling — it's appropriate for all Scout fitness levels with standard preparation.

Right-Sized for Scouts

At 4.8 miles and ~2.5 hrs, this fits perfectly as a half-day hike — leaving time for a second activity (Window Trail, Hot Springs, or stargazing). Good confidence builder before a bigger peak attempt.

The Only Hard Section

Miles 1.2–2.1 are where it earns its moderate-to-strenuous reputation — 0.9 miles of sustained 11–17% grade. The rest of the trail is genuinely manageable for most fitness levels.

Recommended Troop Start Time · Oct 9–13

Depart trailhead by 6:30 AM — earlier than you think is necessary. Parking is the #1 logistical challenge. Sunrise is ~7:20 AM so the first 30 minutes will be pre-dawn cool. Summit by 9:00–9:30 AM, back at trailhead by 11:00 AM. This leaves a full afternoon free — ideal for the Hot Springs or Boquillas Canyon the same day.
